
# Interview Top Total

## Tell me about yourself:

Thank you for the opportunity. I am a Data Scientist with over six years of experience, transitioning from an Economics background 
into advanced machine learning and statistical modeling.

**Past Experience** 

My career has provided me with a diverse foundation, including roles at major financial institutions like Banco Pichincha, where I developed high-impact models for client segmentation and recommendation systems, and experience in the public sector, analyzing risk and social data for institutions like BIESS (Biess, or the Bank of the Ecuadorian Social Security Institute (IESS) - bank dedicated to democratizing access to credit through the contributions of its members.) and the ILO-Chamber of Industries and Production.

**Present Focus** 

Currently, I work as a Data Science Consultant at CGESPLAN, where I focus on developing and presenting data-driven business strategies. I enjoy applying complex statistical methods to transform raw data into actionable business solutions.

**Future/Why Here** 

I am now looking to leverage this comprehensive background in a dynamic environment like Top Total, where I can apply my analytical skills to large-scale challenges and contribute to impactful business decisions.

## Why top total ? 

Toptal's model matches exactly how I work and where I want to go professionally. Throughout my career — from Banco Pichincha to BIESS to several international consultancies — I've consistently worked in high-stakes, project-based environments where the quality bar is high and the impact is measurable. Toptal's reputation for working only with the top tier of talent, on the most important projects of its clients, is precisely the kind of environment where I do my best work.

More specifically, my goal is to expand my reach and work with US-based companies on complex, data-driven challenges. From Ecuador, doing that without the right bridge is difficult. Toptal provides exactly that — direct access to top-tier global clients, with a remote-first model I'm already used to. I've been operating as a consultant across multiple clients simultaneously for several years, so this isn't a new way of working for me; it's a natural next step.

Finally, I think the timing is right for this partnership. Toptal is actively expanding into LATAM, and I bring deep regional expertise — from labor market modeling with the ILO to behavioral credit scoring at Ecuador's largest social security bank. I can deliver high-quality work for US clients while also bringing contextual value that few candidates in this talent pool can offer.

## Questions to ask the recruiter

**About project matching and type of work**

1. *"What kinds of Data Science engagements does Toptal typically match senior candidates with — are most projects in the financial sector, or is the portfolio broad? I ask because my strongest work has been in credit risk, behavioral scoring, and recommendation systems, and I want to understand where I'd add the most value."*
   > Shows domain self-awareness; surfaces whether your niche is in demand on their platform.

2. *"Are most Data Science engagements full-time (40 hrs/week) or part-time? And is it common for senior consultants to run two or three engagements simultaneously?"*
   > You've been a multi-client consultant for years — confirm Toptal's model actually supports that.

3. *"What's the typical engagement length for Data Science projects — weeks, a quarter, or longer-term retainers?"*
   > Helps you plan workload and income predictability.

**About timeline and first placement**

4. *"Once someone passes the full screening, how long does it usually take to land the first project? Is there a bench period, or does matching start immediately?"*
   > Practical: you need to know the real ramp time before income starts.

**About the LATAM expansion**

5. *"I saw that Toptal is expanding into LATAM in 2026. Does that mean there will be more LATAM-based clients to match with, or is the goal still primarily placing LATAM talent with US and European clients?"*
   > Shows you did your homework; also clarifies whether regional expertise opens additional doors for you.

**About rates and billing**

6. *"How does rate negotiation work in practice? Does Toptal set the rate with the client, or do I have visibility and input into what's proposed on my behalf?"*
   > Critical for protecting your $60/hr floor without being blindsided after the process.

**About what makes someone successful**

7. *"What separates the Data Scientists who thrive on Toptal from those who struggle after onboarding? I want to set myself up for a long-term relationship, not just pass the screening."*
   > Signals maturity and seriousness; also genuinely useful intel for you.
